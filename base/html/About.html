My Story

“Will You Share Your Gender Swap Photos with Your Parents”
As a person who has a keen desire to build a society embracing gender minorities and gender diversity, this article posted by Vice caught my eye.
It talked about the untold number of Chinese users used VPN to gain access to the app and share their swapped faces on social networking platforms.
The journalist at Vice took photos for each interviewee with this filter, and probed into their attitudes toward homosexuality, feminism, cross-dressing, personal perception of their own sexuality and gender.
It is exciting to see younger generations are more receptive towards homosexuality and cross-dressing.

This Snapchat filter shed light on my understanding about what technologies could do to change our values, conventions, social norms, and eventually, our society.
And I saw the possibility of pursuing my aspirations in solving social issues through technology.
And by levering this powerful tool, my efforts could probably yield substantial results.

My technical recruiter work provides me a lens to see what are the most popular technologies in the IT industry, and it also allows me to understand their usage and functions.
The increasingly thick atmosphere of innovation in the IT industry in Seattle and daily access to avant-garde technologies helped me cultivated an interest in the IT industry.
I started self-learning via Coursera, and took the Probability and Statistics course offered online by Stanford University.
After enrolled in BU MET CS MSSD, I had in-depth study about software development and is expecting to graduate in this winter semester.
